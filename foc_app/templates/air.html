AIR POLLUTION

INTRODUCTION

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the air that we breathe. These pollutants can come from vehicles, industries, burning waste, and natural sources.

1. What is air pollution?
Air pollution is the contamination of air with harmful gases, smoke, and particles that affect living beings and the environment.

2. How does it destroy our lives?
It causes respiratory diseases, heart problems, reduces air quality, and leads to premature deaths worldwide.

CAUSES

Air pollution is mainly caused by vehicles, factories, burning fossil fuels, and deforestation. These activities release harmful gases like carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen oxides into the atmosphere.

How to Prevent Air Pollution?

Air pollution can be reduced by using public transport, planting trees, reducing waste burning, and switching to renewable energy sources like solar and wind power.
