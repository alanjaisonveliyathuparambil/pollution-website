NOISE POLLUTION

INTRODUCTION

Noise pollution refers to unwanted or harmful sounds that disturb the natural balance of life. It is mainly caused by traffic, industries, construction work, and loud speakers.

1. What is noise pollution?
Noise pollution is excessive sound that causes discomfort and affects human and animal life.

2. How does it destroy our lives?
It leads to hearing loss, stress, sleep disturbance, and increases heart-related health issues.

CAUSES

Noise pollution is mainly caused by vehicles, loud music systems, industrial machines, construction activities, and urban crowding.

How to Prevent Noise Pollution?

Noise pollution can be reduced by using silencers in vehicles, avoiding loud music, planting trees, and following noise regulations in cities.

Noise pollution is harmful sound that disrupts health and the environment. It is managed through three main strategies: Source Control, which involves maintaining machinery and using silencers; Path Intervention, such as installing acoustic barriers or planting Green Muffler tree belts to absorb sound; and Legal Regulation, which enforces strict decibel limits in residential and silence zones near schools or hospitals. Protecting individuals with ear protection and soundproofing also helps mitigate daily impact.
